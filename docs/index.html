María Grandury

Researcher

mariagrandury@gmail.com

María is a Natural Language Processing researcher at the Technical University of Madrid, focused on developing responsible, inclusive, and open artificial intelligence in Spanish. She is the founder of SomosNLP, the largest Spanish-speaking NLP community, and has led the creation of the first benchmark for generative models in Spanish and co-official languages. A Hugging Face Fellow and contributor to international projects like BigScience and Latam-GPT, her research focuses on the evaluation of large language models and their social, cultural, and linguistic biases. She has worked at startups such as Clibrain and neurocat, and collaborates with initiatives that promote women's participation in tech and a more ethical, accessible AI.

Gonzalo Martínez Ruíz

Assistant Professor

gonzmart@pa.uc3m.es

Gonzalo Martínez holds a PhD and is a postdoctoral researcher in Telematics Engineering at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid, specializing in generative AI, communication networks, and cybersecurity. He has contributed to projects like B5G-OPEN, ITACA, and others funded by the Spanish State Research Agency. He has industry experience as a consultant at EY and holds a patent in collaboration with NVIDIA. Gonzalo has published in journals such as IEEE Network and ACM TIST, authored books on ChatGPT, teaches undergraduate and graduate courses, coordinates online LLM courses, and completed a research stay at the University of Edinburgh.

Miguel González

Researcher

miguel.gonsaiz@upm.es

Miguel González is a researcher at the Polytechnic University of Madrid (UPM), specializing in the evaluation of generative Artificial Intelligence models, particularly in Spanish. He holds a Master’s degree in Telecommunications Engineering (2023), Cybersecurity (2022), and a Bachelor’s in Telematics Engineering (2021) from Carlos III University of Madrid. He began his research career in 2022 with the ACHILLES project and currently participates in SMARTY (Horizon Europe), focused on edge AI development. Miguel has collaborated internationally on probabilistic filters and large language models, publishing notable papers. He manages AI training infrastructure at GING and contributes to open-source projects like Chatwords for LLM evaluation.

Irene Plaza

Researcher

irene.plaza.ortiz@alumnos.upm.es

Irene Plaza holds a degree in Telecommunication Engineering from the Universidad Politécnica de Madrid, specializing in telematics, and is currently pursuing a Master's in Cybersecurity at the same university. Her main areas of interest include Artificial Intelligence, Cybersecurity, and Open Data. Her Bachelor's Thesis, focused on benchmarks for evaluating LLMs in Spanish, received the highest distinction and led to the preprint “Spanish and LLM benchmarks: is MMLU lost in translation?”. She is currently a researcher at UPM, contributing to the Eunomia Project funded by INCIBE, which aims to develop open, secure data-sharing solutions that ensure user sovereignty and trust, using FIWARE technologies and interoperability standards.

Cristina Pozo

Researcher

cpozohuertas@gmail.com

Cristina Pozo is a researcher at the Universidad Politécnica de Madrid, where she studies the linguistic knowledge of LLMs. She holds a BA in English Language and Literature from the University of Essex, where her final project was a sociolinguistic study on /s/ weakening in the Spanish of Segovia, based on fieldwork with seventeen interviewees, and graded with distinction. She is currently pursuing a Master’s in Spanish Language Research at the Universidad Autónoma de Madrid. Cristina was part of the editorial team for Creel 5 and Creel 6: Chiaroscuro, contributing text selection, editing, layout, illustration, and cover design. She also writes creatively and studies English (C2 CPE) and Japanese (JLPT N4).
